## Additional Notes

- **Reconstitution & Tracking**: Many peptides come lyophilized. Common vial sizes are 5 mg or 10 mg. Accurate mg/mL calculations and inventory tracking are essential (especially useful in tools like PeptideStack).
- **Administration**: Most research peptides are administered via subcutaneous injection using insulin syringes. Rotate injection sites and maintain sterile technique.
- **Monitoring**: Regular bloodwork (metabolic panels, inflammatory markers, immune function, IGF-1, etc.) is strongly recommended for most peptides.
- **Cycling Philosophy**: Many protocols include on/off periods due to limited long-term human safety data.
- **Sourcing**: Prioritize pharma-grade or high-quality compounding pharmacies when possible. Gray-market sources carry significant purity and contamination risks (as discussed in the episode).
- **Stacks**: Thoughtful combinations (e.g., BPC-157 + TB-500 for healing, or GH secretagogues with metabolic support) are common in practice but require medical oversight.
